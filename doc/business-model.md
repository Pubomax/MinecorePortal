# Business Model & Operating Plan

## Company Type
Methodology‑led premium consulting.

## Core Offer
Core Alignment

## Methodology
The Core Source Model

## Primary Buyer
CEO

## Minimum Engagement
$5,000

## Sales Motion
Selective, form‑only, manual follow‑up.

---

## 1) Business Model (How Money Is Made)
### 1.1 What you sell (very clearly)
You sell structural correction, not services.

Specifically:
- Elimination of operational silos
- Creation of a single source of truth
- Governance that holds under scale

You are paid for alignment, not execution volume.

This removes you from:
- Agencies
- Freelancers
- Tool integrators
- “AI consultants”

### 1.2 Offer stack (intentionally narrow)
**Primary offer (now):**
- Core Alignment
- 6‑month phased engagement
- $5k–$10k+ depending on complexity
- CEO‑only relationship

**Future offers (not now, but structurally allowed):**
- Advanced Integration (post‑alignment)
- Advisory Retainer (governance only)

**Rule:**
No new offer until Core Alignment has been delivered at least 3 times cleanly.

### 1.3 Revenue model
Low volume. High margin. High control.

Early‑stage target:
- 1–2 concurrent clients
- $7,500 average engagement
- Predictable workload
- Zero discounting

Stability‑first, not growth‑first.

---

## 2) Target Market (Who Buys)
### 2.1 Ideal Customer Profile (final)
- Industry: Retail & Services
- Revenue: $1M+ / year
- Team size: 5+
- Buyer: CEO
- Maturity: Automation 3+
- Core pain: Fragmented systems, no single source of truth
- Buying trigger: Growth causing friction, not failure

You do not sell hope. You sell order.

### 2.2 Why they buy from you
They don’t buy because:
- You’re cheaper
- You’re faster
- You use cool tools

They buy because:
- Internal alignment is politically hard
- Hiring internally is expensive and risky
- They want accountability without overhead

Your advantage is structural authority.

---

## 3) Value Proposition (Why You Win)
### 3.1 The core promise (internal)
“We restore clarity and control when growth breaks operations.”

### 3.2 Competitive positioning
You win against:
- Agencies → add tools, not structure
- Internal hires → slower and permanent cost
- Consultants → lack a defined system

You should lose against:
- DIY founders
- Early‑stage startups
- Cost‑sensitive businesses

---

## 4) Sales Model (How Deals Close)
### 4.1 Sales philosophy
Sales is qualification, not persuasion.

One form. One call. One decision.
No pipelines. No nurturing. No chasing.

### 4.2 Conversion expectations (realistic)
- Low lead volume
- High qualification rate
- High close rate on qualified leads
- Calm sales conversations

If volume increases, something broke upstream.

---

## 5) Marketing Model (How Leads Arrive)
### 5.1 Marketing’s only job
Filter out non‑ICP leads before they contact you.

Not to educate. Not to convince. Not to build audience.

### 5.2 Channels (intentionally limited)
- Website (Core Source Model as centerpiece)
- Direct referrals
- Selective outbound (later)
- Ads only when:
  - Sales is boring
  - Delivery is repeatable
  - Capacity rules are enforced

---

## 6) Delivery Model (How Work Is Done)
### 6.1 Delivery philosophy
Phased. Opinionated. Structured. Non‑custom by default.

Customization happens inside phases, not to the model itself.

### 6.2 Capacity rules
- Max 2 concurrent engagements early
- No overlapping diagnostics if it reduces quality
- No “quick asks” outside scope

Protect the system first, always.

---

## 7) Financial Model (What Must Be True)
### 7.1 Financial rules
- Below $5k → no deal
- Discounts → no deal
- Delayed payment → no work
- Scope creep → reset engagement

### 7.2 Sustainability test (monthly)
- Am I calm delivering?
- Am I tempted to add services?
- Am I explaining myself too much?

If yes → something is off.

---

## 8) 90‑Day Business Objectives (Tied to Reality)
By Day 90:
- Core Alignment delivered at least once
- Methodology refined from reality
- Sales conversations feel controlled
- You can say “no” without stress
- Business feels smaller but sharper

That’s success.

---

## 9) What This Business Is Not
Not an agency. Not a volume play. Not a SaaS. Not a personal brand. Not a hustle.

It is a controlled, premium consulting business.

---

## Final Truth
If you execute this plan:
- You won’t grow fast
- You won’t feel busy
- You won’t impress people on Twitter

But you will:
- Make real money
- Keep your sanity
- Build authority
- Have optionality later

That’s the trade.
