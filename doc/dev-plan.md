# Development Plan — 90-Day Operating Plan (Core Alignment)

## Objective
Turn Core Alignment into a repeatable, controlled, premium consulting business.

## Structure
Three phases of 30 days. Each phase has one primary goal, clear actions, and success criteria.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundation & Control (Days 1–30)
**Primary goal:** Create operational clarity and prevent early chaos.

### 1.1 Lock the business operating rules (Week 1)
**Rules (non‑negotiable):**
- One offer only: Core Alignment
- One buyer: CEO
- One floor: $5,000
- One engagement at a time (max 2 if already have bandwidth)
- No discounts, no exceptions
- No work without data access

**Deliverable:**
- 1‑page internal document: “How We Operate”

### 1.2 Financial hygiene (Week 1–2)
**Setup:**
- Separate business account
- Simple accounting tool (Wave, QuickBooks, or equivalent)
- Monthly fixed costs list (including owner salary)

**Track weekly:**
- Cash on hand
- Burn rate
- Open invoices
- Engagement value

**Rule:**
- If you don’t know your cash runway, you don’t get to “think strategy”.

### 1.3 Delivery baseline (Week 2–3)
**Define privately:**
- What happens in the first 2 weeks of Core Alignment
- What data you always request
- What decisions you always force

**Format:**
- Bullet points only (no slides).

### 1.4 Website alignment (Week 3–4)
**Must be true by Day 30:**
- Homepage leads with Core Source Model
- One CTA: “Request Core Alignment Review”
- Methodology page published
- Form includes budget floor, CEO‑only language, data‑sharing requirement

**Constraints:**
- No SEO work yet
- No content marketing yet

**Phase 1 success criteria:**
- You can explain your business in 2 minutes
- You know your monthly burn
- You can say “no” without hesitation
- One serious lead can go end‑to‑end cleanly

---

## Phase 2 — Validation & Delivery (Days 31–60)
**Primary goal:** Prove Core Alignment works in reality, not just in theory.

### 2.1 Close 1–2 Core Alignment engagements
**Targets:**
- 1 closed deal minimum
- 2 maximum (control > volume)

**Sales discipline:**
- One call
- No proposal before diagnosis
- No discount conversation
- No explaining the price
- If someone hesitates on price, disqualify quietly

### 2.2 Deliver deliberately
**During delivery, document:**
- What you actually do
- What feels repetitive
- What feels custom
- Where friction appears

**Rule:**
- No optimization yet; observe first.

### 2.3 Refine the methodology (Week 6–8)
**After the first engagement:**
- What phase took the longest?
- Where did clients push back?
- What assumptions were wrong?

**Adjust:**
- Language
- Phase boundaries
- Expectations

**Guardrails:**
- Do not add services
- Do not lower standards

**Phase 2 success criteria:**
- At least one client completed Phase 1–2 of the model
- Delivery feels calmer than selling
- You can explain the methodology without slides
- You didn’t break your own rules

---

## Phase 3 — Leverage & Confidence (Days 61–90)
**Primary goal:** Turn experience into authority and control (not growth).

### 3.1 Codify what works (Week 9–10)
**Privately document:**
- Client patterns
- Common objections
- Non‑obvious wins
- Red flags missed early

**Use to:**
- Improve qualification
- Strengthen filtering
- Increase sales confidence

### 3.2 Strengthen the sales conversation (Week 10–11)
**Refine:**
- The first 5 minutes of the call
- How you explain the model
- How you handle “why you?”

**Success indicator:**
- You are comfortable losing the deal.

### 3.3 Decide what NOT to do (Week 12)
**Explicitly decide:**
- Which industries you won’t pursue
- Which requests you will always reject
- Which future offers you will not build

**Phase 3 success criteria:**
- You can close without tension
- You can reject without guilt
- Delivery feels structured, not draining
- The business feels smaller but stronger

---

## Outcome at Day 90
- A real offer
- A real methodology
- Paying clients
- Clean rules
- Mental space

You are not “scaled.” You are stable.
